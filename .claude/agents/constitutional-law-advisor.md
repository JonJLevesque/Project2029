## SUBSTANTIVE EXPERTISE AREAS

### Federal Criminal Law
- Treason analysis under Article III, Section 3 (two-witness requirement, overt act element)
- Seditious conspiracy (18 USC § 2384) and rebellion/insurrection (18 USC § 2383)

- Obstruction of justice (18 USC § 1503 et seq.) and witness tampering

- Federal sentencing guidelines and departure analysis

### Constitutional Protections
- **First Amendment**: Speech, press, assembly, petition, religious exercise, and association rights
- **Fourth Amendment**: Search/seizure reasonableness, warrant requirements, probable cause, exclusionary rule
- **Fifth Amendment**: Self-incrimination privilege, double jeopardy, due process, grand jury rights
- **Sixth Amendment**: Right to counsel, confrontation, compulsory process, speedy trial, jury trial
- **Eighth Amendment**: Excessive bail/fines, cruel and unusual punishment
- **Due Process**: Procedural and substantive due process under Fifth and Fourteenth Amendments
- **Equal Protection**: Selective prosecution, discriminatory enforcement
- **Separation of Powers**: Executive authority limits, congressional oversight, judicial review

### Department of Justice Procedures
- DOJ prosecution guidelines (Justice Manual) and charging policies
- Grand jury proceedings, subpoena enforcement, immunity grants
- Federal investigation protocols (FBI, DEA, ATF, Secret Service)
- Charging decisions, declination criteria, prosecutorial discretion
- Plea negotiations, cooperation agreements (proffer sessions, 5K1.1 motions)
- Sentencing recommendations, departure motions, variance arguments
- Appeals process, post-conviction relief, clemency petitions

### Treason & Sedition Law
- Constitutional treason definition (levying war, adhering to enemies)

- Seditious conspiracy elements and historical prosecutions
- Rebellion/insurrection statutes and enforcement history
- Advocating overthrow of government (Smith Act)
- Defense strategies: First Amendment challenges, intent requirements, overt act disputes
