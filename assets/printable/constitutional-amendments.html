Project 2029: Proposed Constitutional Amendments

7 Amendments to Perfect the Union -- Building the Democracy We Have Never Had
"Democracy is not inevitable. It is a choice that must be made by every generation." -- These amendments represent a 10-20 year project to structurally reform American democracy.
28th
Voting Rights Amendment
Establish a universal, affirmative right to vote for every U.S. citizen aged 18+.
  • Constitutionalize the right to vote (currently implied, not explicit)
  • Prohibit all voter suppression tactics: strict ID laws, purges, long lines
  • Mandate automatic voter registration nationwide
  • Require same-day registration and early voting in all states
Chapter 29 -- Structural Safeguards
29th
Presidential Accountability
Close the self-pardon loophole and strengthen presidential accountability.
  • Explicitly prohibit self-pardons by any sitting president
  • Limit pardon power for co-conspirators and family members
  • Require disclosure of all pardon deliberations
  • Clarify that no president is above criminal law
Chapter 29 -- Structural Safeguards
30th
Citizens United Reversal
Overturn Citizens United: money is not speech, corporations are not people.
  • Allow Congress and states to regulate campaign spending
  • End unlimited corporate and dark money in elections
  • Restore pre-2010 campaign finance regulation authority
  • Interim: DISCLOSE Act, super PAC limits, DOJ enforcement unit (50 attorneys)
Chapter 30 -- Fundamental Transformation (Reform I)
31st
Senate Term Limits
Limit U.S. Senators to two terms (12 years maximum service).
  • End lifelong political careers in the upper chamber
  • Increase turnover, diversity, and fresh perspectives
  • Reduce accumulation of special interest power
  • Applies prospectively to future elections
Chapter 30 -- Fundamental Transformation (Reform II)
32nd
Electoral College Abolition
Elect the President by direct national popular vote.
  • Every vote counts equally regardless of state
  • Majority requirement with instant runoff
  • End "swing state" dominance of campaigns
  • Interim: National Popular Vote Interstate Compact
Chapter 30 -- Fundamental Transformation (Reform III)
33rd
Supreme Court Term Limits
Replace lifetime appointments with 18-year staggered terms.
  • One appointment every 2 years (predictable, regular)
  • Senior status after 18 years (not forced retirement)
  • Binding, enforceable ethics code for all justices
  • Reduces stakes of any single appointment; increases legitimacy
Chapter 29 / Chapter 30 -- Reform XIII
34th
Social Security and Economic Rights Protection
Constitutionally protect Social Security, Medicare, and core economic rights.
  • Enshrine Social Security and Medicare as constitutional rights
  • Prohibit benefit cuts below current levels without supermajority
 
  • Establish right to healthcare, education, and housing as state obligations
  • Create constitutional floor for economic dignity and security

The Amendment Process -- Article V of the Constitution

1
Proposal
2/3 of both chambers of Congress (290 House, 67 Senate) OR a convention called by 2/3 of state legislatures (34 states)
▶
2
Ratification
3/4 of state legislatures must ratify (38 out of 50 states) -- no presidential signature required
▶
3
Adoption
Amendment becomes part of the Constitution -- the supreme law of the land, binding on all branches and states
Years 1-2
Build grassroots movement, draft language
Years 3-5
Congressional proposal, public education
Years 5-10
State-by-state ratification campaigns
Years 10-20
Full adoption and consolidation
