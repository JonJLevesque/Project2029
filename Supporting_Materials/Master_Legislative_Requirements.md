## ๐จ YEAR 1 PRIORITY LEGISLATION (First 100 Days)

### DEMOCRACY & ELECTIONS

**1. Voting Rights Advancement Act** (Ch 29)
- **Purpose:** Restore and strengthen VRA preclearance
- **Key Provisions:**
  - Restore Section 5 preclearance (Shelby County fix)
  - Prohibit voter purges within 90 days of elections
  - Same-day registration requirement
  - 2-week early voting minimum mandate
  - Vote-by-mail option for all
  - Automatic voter registration (AVR)
  - Polling place adequacy standards
- **Timeline:** Pass within 100 days
- **Enforcement:** DOJ Civil Rights Division
- **Budget:** $500M annually for enforcement + grants

**2. Election Security Act** (Ch 29)
- **Purpose:** Secure elections from interference
- **Key Provisions:**
  - Paper ballot requirements (all electronic must have trail)
  - Auditable systems mandatory
  - Foreign interference prohibition enforcement
  - Campaign finance disclosure enhancement
  - Disinformation countermeasures
  - State election security funding ($1B)
- **Timeline:** Year 1
- **Budget:** $1B initial, $250M/year ongoing

**3. For the People Act (Enhanced)** (Ch 29)
- **Purpose:** Comprehensive democracy reform
- **Key Provisions:**
  - Automatic voter registration
  - Independent redistricting commissions
  - Campaign finance transparency
  - Ethics reforms
  - Voting access expansion
- **Timeline:** Year 1
- **Status:** Builds on HR 1/S 1

### ANTI-CORRUPTION

**4. Lobbying Prohibition and Transparency Act** (Ch 30, Ch 17)
- **Purpose:** Ban revolving door, drastically limit lobbying
- **Key Provisions:**
  - **Members of Congress:** Lifetime ban on lobbying former chamber, 10-year ban federally
  - **Senior Staff:** 10-year cooling-off for Congress, 5-year for executive
  - **Cabinet/White House:** 10-year ban on lobbying any government
  - **Absolute gift ban** (zero tolerance)
  - Real-time disclosure (48 hours)
  - Foreign lobbying permanently banned for officials
  - Office of Public Integrity created (independent enforcement)
- **Criminal Penalties:** Up to 5 years, $100K+ fines
- **Budget:** $500M for enforcement agency
- **Timeline:** Year 1 (priority)

**5. DISCLOSE Act (Enhanced)** (Ch 30, Ch 17)
- **Purpose:** Campaign finance transparency while Citizens United stands
- **Key Provisions:**
  - All spending over $10,000 disclosed within 48 hours
  - Beneficial ownership transparency (no shell companies)
  - Foreign money prohibition enforcement
  - "Stand By Your Ad" for all spending
  - Criminal penalties for violations
- **Enforcement:** DOJ Campaign Finance Unit (50 attorneys)
- **Budget:** $75M annually
- **Timeline:** Year 1

**6. Congressional Accountability and Ethics Commission Act** (Ch 30)
- **Purpose:** Create independent Congressional ethics enforcement
- **Key Provisions:**
  - 9-member external commission
  - Appointed by President (3), Chief Justice (3), Comptroller General (3)
  - Investigative authority (subpoena power)
  - Civil penalties up to $500K
  - Can recommend expulsion (Congress votes)
  - Mandatory referrals for crimes
- **Budget:** $100M annually
- **Timeline:** Year 1

### DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE REFORM

**7. Department of Justice Independence Act** (Ch 17)
- **Purpose:** Insulate DOJ from political interference
- **Key Provisions:**
  - Attorney General 10-year fixed term (removable only for cause)
  - 60-vote Senate confirmation requirement
  - White House contact restrictions (logged, reported to Congress)
  - Criminal penalties for improper influence
  - Budget requests direct to Congress
- **Timeline:** Year 1
- **Constitutional Note:** Some provisions may face challenge

**8. Public Integrity Enhancement Act** (Ch 17, Ch 28)
- **Purpose:** Strengthen corruption enforcement
- **Key Provisions:**
  - Campaign finance violations prosecution
  - Lobbying law enforcement
  - Ethics violations authority
  - Bribery and gratuities enforcement
  - **Campaign Finance Enforcement Unit:** 50 attorneys (DOJ)
  - **Lobbying Enforcement Unit:** coordination with Office of Public Integrity
- **Budget:** $100M annually
- **Timeline:** Year 1

### MEDIA & INFORMATION

**9. Digital Era Fairness and Accountability Act** (Ch 8, Ch 30)
- **Purpose:** Restore Fairness Doctrine for modern media
- **Key Provisions:**
  - FCC restore Fairness Doctrine for broadcast (immediate)
  - Extend to cable news channels with "News" in name
  - Digital platform news curation transparency
  - Algorithm audits (no systematic bias)
  - Balanced feed option required
  - Press credentialing standards
- **Budget:** $50M for FCC enforcement
- **Timeline:** Year 1 (FCC rule immediate, legislation for cable/digital)
- **Constitutional Risk:** High (First Amendment challenges likely)

**10. Political Advertising Transparency and Fairness Act** (Ch 29, Ch 30)
- **Purpose:** Ban micro-targeted political ads
- **Key Provisions:**
  - Prohibition on psychographic profiling for politics
  - Ads must target broadly (100K+ people minimum)
  - Ban purchase of psychographic data for campaigns
  - All political ads in public database within 24 hours
  - Platform accountability (verify identity, label content)
  - Enforcement: FTC + FEC, $10M per violation
- **Budget:** $75M enforcement
- **Timeline:** Year 1

### DEMOCRACY TRANSFORMATION

**11. Democracy Funding Act** (Ch 30)
- **Purpose:** Public financing of elections

**12. Ranked-Choice Voting Act** (Ch 30)
- **Purpose:** Mandate RCV for all federal elections
- **Key Provisions:**
  - RCV for President, Senate, House
  - Nonpartisan top-5 primaries (or top-4)
  - Federal funding for implementation ($500M)
  - Technical assistance for states
  - 4-year implementation timeline
- **Timeline:** Pass Year 1, implement by 2028 elections

**13. Federal Law Sunset and Review Act** (Ch 30)
- **Purpose:** Every law reviewed/renewed every 20 years
- **Key Provisions:**
  - All laws subject to sunset (except protected list)
  - 20-year cycle, phased implementation
  - Protected: civil rights, constitutional structure, treaty obligations
  - Committee review years 18-20, vote on renewal
  - Expedited process for routine renewals
- **Timeline:** Year 1 pass, phased implementation over 20 years

## ๐ CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENTS (5-10 Year Ratification Campaigns)

**29. 28th Amendment: Voting Rights** (Ch 29)
```
Section 1: Right to vote shall not be denied or abridged.
Section 2: Congress shall establish national minimum voting standards.
Section 3: No discriminatory practices (race, age 18+, economic status).
Section 4: Early voting, vote by mail, same-day registration required.
```
- **Timeline:** Ratification Years 3-7
- **Status:** Bipartisan support possible

**30. 29th Amendment: Presidential Accountability** (Ch 29)
```
Section 1: No person immune from criminal prosecution for acts as President.
Section 2: Sitting President may be indicted, tried, convicted.
Section 3: Self-pardons prohibited.
```
- **Timeline:** Ratification Years 3-7

**31. 30th Amendment: Overturn Citizens United** (Ch 30)
```
Section 1: Constitutional rights are for natural persons only (not corporations).
Section 2: Government shall regulate campaign contributions/expenditures.
Section 3: All contributions publicly disclosed.
Section 4: Congress and states have enforcement power.
```
- **Timeline:** Ratification Years 3-10
- **Status:** 75%+ public support

**32. 31st Amendment: Senate Term Limits** (Ch 30)
```
Section 1: No person shall serve more than two full terms as Senator (12 years).
Section 2: Does not apply to current senators (grandfathers in).
```
- **Timeline:** Ratification Years 5-10

**33. 32nd Amendment: Abolish Electoral College** (Ch 30)
```
Section 1: President elected by direct popular vote.
Section 2: Majority required; if none, runoff between top two within 60 days.
Section 3: Congress shall establish uniform national election standards.
```
- **Timeline:** Ratification Years 5-12
- **Interim:** National Popular Vote Interstate Compact (NPVIC) - 61 more electoral votes needed

**34. 33rd Amendment: Supreme Court Term Limits** (Ch 29, Ch 30)
```
Section 1: Justices serve 18-year terms (non-renewable).
Section 2: President appoints new Justice every two years.
Section 3: After 18 years, Justice assumes senior status.
Section 4: Congress may prescribe appointment procedures.
```
- **Timeline:** Ratification Years 5-10
- **Note:** Statutory version likely unconstitutional, amendment needed

**35. 34th Amendment: Wealth Tax Authority** (Ch 22)
```
Section 1: The Congress shall have power to lay and collect taxes on wealth,
from whatever source held, without apportionment among the several States.
Section 2: Congress may define wealth to include all forms of property,
assets, and beneficial interests.
Section 3: This article shall not affect the validity of any income tax
authorized by the sixteenth article of amendment.
```
- **Timeline:** Ratification Years 5-10
- **Note:** Backup if courts strike down statutory wealth tax
- **Purpose:** Resolve constitutional ambiguity about direct taxes
