# Opposition Playbook: Anticipate, Counter, Win

## Purpose of This Document

**Know their playbook before they run it.**

This document anticipates every major attack opponents will use against Project 2029 reforms—and provides proven counter-arguments. When you can **predict and preempt** their attacks, you:

1. **Defuse their messaging** before it takes hold
2. **Control the narrative** by framing the debate on your terms
3. **Demonstrate credibility** by showing you've thought through objections
4. **Turn their attacks into opportunities** to reinforce your message

**How to use this playbook:**
- Read the attacks for your issue area before advocacy/debate
- Use the "Call it out first" strategy to inoculate your audience
- Deploy the counters when opponents make these attacks
- Study the "Why they say this" sections to understand their strategy

## 1. Universal Opposition Tactics

### These attacks work across ALL reforms. Master the counters.

---

#### ATTACK: "This is socialism/communism!"

**Why they say this:** Socialism is a boogeyman word that scares older voters. They use it to avoid debating the actual policy.

**Counter:**
> "Social Security is socialism by that definition—and 90% of Americans support it. Medicare is socialism—and seniors love it. Public schools, fire departments, the military—all 'socialism' by your definition. This isn't about socialism vs capitalism. It's about whether our democracy works for everyone or just billionaires. Let's debate the actual policy, not scary labels."

**Call it out first:**
> "Opponents will call this 'socialism' because they can't defend the status quo on the merits. When your only argument is name-calling, you've already lost the debate."

---

#### ATTACK: "You're being too partisan/divisive!"

**Why they say this:** Tone policing. They want you to self-censor while they continue destroying democracy.

**Counter:**
> "Partisan? 70% of Americans—including Republicans—want to overturn Citizens United. 60% want term limits for Supreme Court justices. These reforms have BIPARTISAN public support. The only people who oppose them are the politicians and billionaires benefiting from corruption. If wanting a government that works for all Americans instead of just the wealthy is 'partisan,' then I'm guilty."

**Call it out first:**
> "When they accuse you of being 'divisive,' ask yourself: divisive to whom? To the billionaires buying elections? To the politicians taking corporate money? The real division is between the corrupt establishment and the American people."

---

#### ATTACK: "This will hurt the economy/kill jobs!"

**Why they say this:** Fear-mongering. They claim ANY reform will cause economic collapse.

**Counter:**
> "They said the same thing about ending child labor, the 40-hour workweek, Social Security, Medicare, and the minimum wage. They're always wrong. Countries with stronger social safety nets, higher taxes on the wealthy, and better democratic institutions have STRONGER economies and HIGHER quality of life. Denmark, Norway, Germany—all outperform us on economic mobility, happiness, and median wages. Taking power from billionaires and giving it to working people GROWS the economy."

**Call it out first:**
> "Whenever you threaten the wealth and power of the elite, they predict economic doom. It's the oldest trick in the book—and it's never been true."

---

#### ATTACK: "This is too radical/extreme!"

**Why they say this:** Moving the Overton window. Make reasonable reforms seem unreasonable.

**Counter:**
> "Radical? What's radical is letting billionaires buy elections. What's extreme is lifetime Supreme Court appointments. What's insane is pastors flying private jets bought with tax-exempt donations. Every other advanced democracy has publicly funded elections, proportional representation, and wealth taxes—and they're doing BETTER than us. The status quo is the radical position."

**Reframe:**
> "You know what's extreme? A system where 3 justices appointed by presidents who lost the popular vote can overturn rights for 330 million people. We're not being radical—we're catching up to the rest of the civilized world."

---

#### ATTACK: "The Founding Fathers wouldn't want this!"

**Why they say this:** Worship of founding mythology to block progress.

**Counter:**
> "The Founding Fathers owned slaves, denied women the vote, and limited voting to white male property owners. They created the Electoral College to protect slavery. I'm not interested in what slaveholders wanted—I'm interested in creating a democracy that actually works. Besides, they built in the amendment process SPECIFICALLY so we could improve the Constitution. Using it is honoring their vision, not betraying it."

**Call it out first:**
> "Opponents will invoke the Founding Fathers like they're sacred prophets. Remember: those same Founders created a system that allowed slavery, denied women rights, and restricted voting to wealthy white men. Maybe we shouldn't treat their every decision as infallible."

---

#### ATTACK: "You just want power for your side!"

**Why they say this:** Projection. They assume everyone is as power-hungry as they are.

**Counter:**
> "If we just wanted power for our side, we'd keep the Electoral College (which benefits Republicans), gerrymandering (which both parties use), and unlimited dark money (which we could use too). Instead, we're proposing reforms that make the system fair for EVERYONE: ranked-choice voting, publicly funded elections, proportional representation, independent redistricting. These reforms help whoever wins the most votes—regardless of party. That's called democracy."

**Reframe:**
> "The question isn't 'who benefits?'—it's 'is it fair?' We're not asking for rules that favor Democrats. We're asking for rules that favor voters."

---

#### ATTACK: "This is unprecedented/dangerous!"

**Why they say this:** Appeal to tradition. Change = scary.

**Counter:**
> "So was ending slavery. So was women's suffrage. So was Social Security. Every major advancement in democracy was 'unprecedented' until it wasn't. And you know what's ACTUALLY dangerous? A Supreme Court that can overturn 50 years of precedent on abortion. A president who can pardon co-conspirators. Billionaires who can buy elections. The status quo is the danger."

**Call it out first:**
> "Whenever the powerful face accountability, they call it 'dangerous.' It's dangerous to their power—which is exactly why we need it."

## 2. Reform-Specific Attacks & Counters

---

<a id="citizens-united-reversal"></a>

### Citizens United Reversal

#### ATTACK: "Corporations have free speech rights!"

**Why they say this:** Conflating corporate rights with human rights.

**Counter:**
> "Corporations aren't people. They don't vote, they don't die, they don't go to war. The Founding Fathers didn't write 'We the Corporations.' Money isn't speech—it's money. If money is speech, then poor people don't have free speech and billionaires have a billion times more speech than you. That's not freedom—that's oligarchy."

**The turn:**
> "You know who DOESN'T have free speech right now? The 99% of Americans whose voices are drowned out by billions in corporate spending. We're not limiting speech—we're amplifying it."

---

#### ATTACK: "You can't limit political spending—it's unconstitutional!"

**Counter:**
> "It was constitutional for 100 years. From 1907 (Tillman Act) to 2010 (Citizens United), corporate money in elections was restricted. Citizens United was a 5-4 decision by partisan judges—not sacred text. We've overturned bad Supreme Court decisions before (Dred Scott, Plessy v Ferguson, Korematsu). We can do it again."

---

#### ATTACK: "Unions spend money on politics too!"

**Why they say this:** Whataboutism. False equivalency between unions and corporations.

**Counter:**
> "Union political spending: ~$1 billion/year, funded by workers pooling small donations. Corporate political spending: ~$10+ billion/year, funded by profits extracted from workers. Also, our amendment limits BOTH corporate and union treasury spending equally. But nice try equating worker organizations with multinational corporations."

---

<a id="publicly-funded-elections"></a>

### Publicly Funded Elections

#### ATTACK: "Why should my tax dollars fund candidates I disagree with?!"

**Why they say this:** Individualistic framing. Ignores systemic corruption.

**Counter:**
> "Your tax dollars ALREADY fund corruption. Corporations donate to politicians, get favorable laws, then raise your prices and cut your wages. That costs you THOUSANDS per year. $100/year for democracy vouchers—that YOU control and give to candidates YOU support—is a bargain. Plus, you're already paying for it. We're just cutting out the corporate middleman."

**The turn:**
> "You're not funding candidates you disagree with—you're funding a system where YOUR voice matters as much as a billionaire's. Right now, you're funding a system where you have ZERO voice."

---

#### ATTACK: "This gives politicians free money!"

**Counter:**
> "Politicians get 'free money' NOW—from billionaires and corporations who expect favors in return. At least with publicly funded elections, the money comes with NO STRINGS ATTACHED. Candidates answer to voters, not donors. That's the whole point."

---

#### ATTACK: "This is too expensive!"

**Counter:**
> "The NYC democracy voucher program costs $100 million for 5 million voters = $20/person. Federal program: $200/voter × 150 million voters = $30 billion. Sounds like a lot? We spend $800+ billion on defense. Corporate tax dodging costs us $200+ billion/year. We can afford this—easily. The question is: do we want a democracy or an oligarchy?"

---

<a id="electoral-college-abolition"></a>

### Electoral College Abolition

#### ATTACK: "Small states will be ignored!"

**Why they say this:** Misdirection. Small states are ALREADY ignored.

**Counter:**
> "Small states are ignored NOW. When's the last time a presidential candidate campaigned in Wyoming, Vermont, or Montana? The Electoral College makes candidates focus on 6-8 swing states and ignore the other 42. With a national popular vote, EVERY vote in EVERY state matters equally. A vote in California counts the same as a vote in Iowa. That's democracy."

**The data:**
> "In 2020, candidates spent 95% of their ad money in just 8 states. 42 states got ZERO campaign visits. The Electoral College doesn't protect small states—it ignores them."

---

#### ATTACK: "This gives power to cities/coastal elites!"

**Counter:**
> "Why should WHERE you live determine how much your vote counts? A farmer in rural Pennsylvania and a teacher in Philadelphia should have EQUAL votes—not one worth more than the other. 'Coastal elites?' There are more Republicans in California than in Texas. More Democrats in Texas than in New York. Right now, all those voters are ignored because their states aren't competitive. National popular vote makes EVERYONE count."

---

#### ATTACK: "The Founders created the Electoral College for a reason!"

**Counter:**
> "Yes—to protect slavery. Small states demanded extra power because enslaved people (who couldn't vote) counted toward their Electoral College votes via the Three-Fifths Compromise. It was a racist compromise that let slaveholders punch above their weight. We ended slavery. Time to end its vestiges too."

**Alternative counter:**
> "The Founders also limited voting to white male property owners. Should we keep that too? The Constitution has an amendment process for a reason—to fix mistakes."

---

<a id="supreme-court-term-limits"></a>

### Supreme Court Term Limits

#### ATTACK: "Lifetime appointments ensure judicial independence!"

**Counter:**
> "How's that working out? 5 justices appointed by presidents who LOST the popular vote just overturned Roe, gutted the Voting Rights Act, and legalized corruption. That's not independence—that's partisan capture. 18-year terms with staggered appointments ensure NO president can stack the court and EVERY president gets equal appointments. That's ACTUAL independence."

---

#### ATTACK: "This is court-packing!"

**Counter:**
> "Court-packing is what Mitch McConnell did: blocking Merrick Garland for a year, then rushing Amy Coney Barrett through in 8 days. Term limits PREVENT packing by ensuring predictable, regular turnover. Every president gets 2 appointments per term—no more, no less. That's the OPPOSITE of packing."

---

#### ATTACK: "You just don't like the current court's decisions!"

**Counter:**
> "I don't like that 3 justices appointed by presidents who lost the popular vote can overturn rights for 330 million people for 40+ years. That's not democracy—it's judicial tyranny. Term limits have NOTHING to do with the current court's ideology. They fix a structural flaw that lets random timing and partisan gamesmanship determine constitutional law for generations."

---

<a id="church-taxation-reform"></a>

### Church Taxation Reform

#### ATTACK: "You're attacking religious freedom!"

**Why they say this:** Conflating accountability with persecution.

**Counter:**
> "Taxing megachurch pastors' private jets isn't attacking religion—it's ending abuse. Your local church that feeds the homeless? Still tax-exempt. Kenneth Copeland's $50 million mansion? Taxed. Joel Osteen's $10 million salary? Taxed. We're not targeting faith—we're targeting fraud."

**The turn:**
> "You know who this HELPS? Actual religious communities who follow Jesus's teachings about helping the poor. Right now, prosperity gospel scammers make legitimate ministries look bad. We're separating churches from businesses masquerading as churches."

---

#### ATTACK: "This violates the First Amendment!"

**Counter:**
> "The First Amendment says 'Congress shall make no law respecting an establishment of religion'—it says NOTHING about tax exemptions. Tax exemptions were created by Congress. They can be conditioned by Congress. Churches don't have a constitutional right to tax-free private jets—they have a constitutional right to exist, worship, and preach. No one's stopping that."

---

#### ATTACK: "You're just going after Christians!"

**Counter:**
> "This applies to ALL religious organizations equally: megachurches, televangelists, mosques, synagogues, temples. ANY religious organization that pays its leaders excessive salaries, owns luxury assets, or engages in political campaigning loses exemptions. We're enforcing the rules ALREADY on the books that the IRS has refused to enforce."

<a id="wealth-tax--billionaire-minimum-tax"></a>

### Wealth Tax & Billionaire Minimum Tax

#### ATTACK: "This is punishing success!"

**Why they say this:** Reframing taxation as punishment rather than contribution.

**Counter:**
> "No one earning $50 million pays this tax. This affects 0.01% of Americans—people whose wealth often comes from exploiting workers, dodging taxes, and rigging the system. Meanwhile, teachers, nurses, and firefighters pay 20-30% of their income in taxes while billionaires pay 8% or less. We're not punishing success—we're ending a rigged game."

**The turn:**
> "You want to talk about punishing success? How about punishing workers with stagnant wages while CEO pay increased 1,300%? That's punishment."

---

#### ATTACK: "Wealth taxes don't work—look at Europe!"

**Counter:**
> "European wealth taxes failed because they were VOLUNTARY—rich people could just move to another EU country to avoid them. That's not possible with a U.S. wealth tax because: (1) We tax citizenship, not residency; (2) Renouncing citizenship triggers exit taxes on all wealth; (3) It's a federal tax—no state to escape to. Also, France's wealth tax raised $4+ billion/year before repeal. 'Doesn't work' = still raised billions."

---

#### ATTACK: "They'll just hide their wealth/leave the country!"

**Counter:**
> "We have exit taxes. Renounce citizenship? Pay tax on all unrealized gains immediately. Hide wealth? That's tax fraud—jail time. We track assets through third-party reporting: banks, brokers, property records. And where would they go? The U.S. has the strongest property rights, most stable financial system, and best rule of law. Billionaires aren't moving to Russia or Saudi Arabia to avoid a 3% tax."

---

#### ATTACK: "This will hurt innovation/entrepreneurship!"

**Counter:**
> "The highest marginal tax rate in U.S. history was 91% (1950s)—and that's when we had the MOST innovation: interstate highways, space program, medical breakthroughs, tech boom. High taxes on extreme wealth don't stop innovation—they FUND it through public investment. Plus, entrepreneurs don't start businesses to become billionaires—they do it to create, build, and solve problems. A 3% wealth tax on $1 billion ($30M/year) still leaves you with $970 million. You'll survive."

<a id="fairness-doctrine-restoration"></a>

### Fairness Doctrine Restoration

#### ATTACK: "This is censorship/violates free speech!"

**Why they say this:** Conflating regulation with censorship.

**Counter:**
> "The Fairness Doctrine was in place for 40 years—and it was upheld as constitutional by the Supreme Court (Red Lion v FCC, 1969). It's not censorship to require broadcast media—which use PUBLIC airwaves—to present multiple viewpoints. You can still say whatever you want. You just can't use public resources to broadcast ONE viewpoint 24/7 while ignoring all others."

**The turn:**
> "You know what ACTUALLY limits speech? Media monopolies that give 95% of airtime to one perspective and blacklist the other 5%. The Fairness Doctrine EXPANDS speech by ensuring diverse viewpoints."

---

#### ATTACK: "The market will sort it out!"

**Counter:**
> "The market gave us Fox News, OAN, and Newsmax on one side, MSNBC on the other—with no incentive for truth, only for outrage. When profit comes from polarization, the 'market solution' is more polarization. The Fairness Doctrine existed BECAUSE the market failed. We tried the market approach for 35 years—it gave us the most divided, misinformed electorate in U.S. history."

---

#### ATTACK: "This only targets conservative media!"

**Counter:**
> "It applies to ALL broadcast media equally: Fox, MSNBC, CNN, everyone. If you think only conservative media violates fairness standards, that tells you something about conservative media—not about the Fairness Doctrine."

<a id="universal-basic-services"></a>

### Universal Basic Services

#### ATTACK: "We can't afford this!"

**Why they say this:** Scarcity framing. Pretend resources don't exist.

**Counter:**
> "We're the richest country in human history. We CAN afford it—we're choosing not to. We spent $8 trillion on wars in the Middle East. We give $800+ billion/year to the military. Billionaires increased their wealth by $2.1 trillion during COVID. The money exists—we're just letting billionaires hoard it instead of investing it in our people."

**The reframe:**
> "The question isn't 'can we afford it?' It's 'can we afford NOT to?' Medical bankruptcies cost the economy $200+ billion/year. Student debt crisis costs $100+ billion in lost economic activity. Unaffordable housing costs $200+ billion in homelessness, reduced productivity, and crime. FIXING these problems MAKES money."

---

#### ATTACK: "This will make people lazy!"

**Counter:**
> "Every country with universal healthcare, free college, and stronger social safety nets has HIGHER workforce participation than the U.S. Denmark, Norway, Germany, Finland—all have better outcomes AND more people working. Why? Because when people aren't terrified of bankruptcy or homelessness, they take risks, start businesses, change careers, get education. Security ENABLES productivity—it doesn't kill it."

**The data:**
> "States that expanded Medicaid saw INCREASED workforce participation. Why? Because healthy people work. People not bankrupted by medical bills work. Turns out, helping people helps the economy."

---

#### ATTACK: "This is socialism!"

**Counter (see universal tactics above, but specific version):**
> "Medicare is 'socialism'—and seniors love it because it WORKS. Public schools are 'socialism'—and they built the American middle class. Fire departments are 'socialism'—and they save lives. Stop using scary labels and debate the actual policy: should healthcare, education, and housing be human rights or profit centers for corporations? Every other developed nation chose rights. We chose profits. How's that working out?"

### Legal Accountability (Chapter 28)

We have him on video inciting a mob that attacked the Capitol. This isn't political—it's criminal. If you or I did ANY of those things, we'd be in prison. Equal justice under law means EVERYONE follows the law—including presidents."

**Call it out first:**

Violating the Constitution isn't a 'political dispute'—it's a betrayal of oath. We're not prosecuting people for their politics—we're prosecuting them for crimes. There's a difference."

#### ATTACK: "This will set a dangerous precedent!"

**Counter:**
> "You know what sets a dangerous precedent? NOT holding coup plotters accountable. After Reconstruction failed to prosecute Confederate leaders, they returned to power and created Jim Crow. After Iran-Contra pardons, the same people returned and led us into Iraq. After torture went unpunished, abuses continued. IMPUNITY sets the precedent—accountability BREAKS it."

**The turn:**

Trump destroyed norms when he pardoned co-conspirators, ignored subpoenas, and incited an insurrection. Republicans destroyed the norm against voter suppression, gerrymandering, and unlimited dark money. We're not destroying norms—we're fighting back against people who already destroyed them."

**The turn:**

---

#### ATTACK: "This is just as bad as what Trump did!"

McConnell packed courts to entrench minority rule. We expand courts to restore majority rule. Trump declared fake emergencies to build a wall. We declare real emergencies to stop climate change. The difference is: we're using power for democracy—they're using it against democracy."

But no more winning the presidency while losing the popular vote. No more gerrymandering to hold the House with 45% of votes. No more unlimited corporate money buying elections. The rules apply to EVERYONE—including us."

#### ATTACK: "You're being hypocrites!"

**Counter:**
> "Hypocrisy would be opposing these tactics when Republicans use them, then using them ourselves. We're being CONSISTENT: we opposed Republicans using these tactics for authoritarian ends, and we support using them for democratic ends. The tactics aren't the problem—the ENDS are. Republicans used power to restrict voting, overturn elections, and entrench minority rule. We're using power to expand voting, protect elections, and restore majority rule. That's not hypocrisy—that's moral clarity."

## 3. Rhetorical Trap Questions

### These are designed to make you look bad no matter how you answer. Here's how to escape the trap:

---

#### TRAP: "So you want to punish people for being successful?"

**The trap:** If you say yes, you look anti-success. If you say no, they claim you don't support your own policy.

**Escape:**
> "I want everyone to contribute fairly. Right now, billionaires pay 8% in taxes while teachers pay 25%. That's not 'success'—it's a rigged game. I want to reward work and innovation—not tax dodging and exploitation."

**Then turn it around:**
> "Let me ask YOU: do you think it's fair that a nurse pays higher tax rates than a billionaire? Because if you defend that, you're punishing hard work."

---

#### TRAP: "Where do you draw the line?"

**The trap:** Designed to make any line seem arbitrary.

**Escape:**
> "We draw the line based on data and outcomes. Wealth tax kicks in at $50 million because that's where wealth accumulation becomes detached from productivity and starts reflecting systemic advantages. Why 18-year Supreme Court terms? Because it ensures every president gets 2 appointments per term—fair and predictable. These aren't arbitrary—they're evidence-based."

---

#### TRAP: "If this is so great, why hasn't it been done before?"

**The trap:** Assumes status quo = best option.

**Escape:**
> "That's what they said about abolishing slavery, women's suffrage, Social Security, and Medicare. 'If it's so great, why haven't we done it before?' Because entrenched power resists change. Every major democratic advancement faced this question—and every time, the answer was: 'Because the people who benefit from the current system have the power to stop it.' Until they don't."

---

#### TRAP: "What about [extreme hypothetical scenario]?"

**The trap:** Force you to defend against absurd edge case.

**Example:** "What if someone has all their wealth in art—do you force them to sell their Picassos to pay the wealth tax?!"

**Escape:**
> "That's why the policy includes specific provisions for illiquid assets: pay in installments, defer until sale, or donate to charity for credit. These edge cases are solved in the bill text. But nice try avoiding the actual debate: should billionaires pay lower tax rates than nurses? Yes or no?"

---

#### TRAP: "Aren't you just as corrupt/partisan as them?"

**The trap:** False equivalence. Makes you defensive.

**Escape:**
> "Let's compare: They took $1 billion+ in corporate PAC money. We're banning corporate PAC money. They appointed judges to overturn elections. We're creating independent courts. They suppressed votes. We're expanding voting rights. If fighting corruption makes us 'partisan,' then yeah—we're partisan for democracy. What are YOU partisan for?"

<a id="how-to-inoculate-your-audience"></a>

## 4. How to Inoculate Your Audience

### The "Call It Out First" Strategy

When you **predict their attacks before they make them**, you:
1. **Defuse the attack** (people dismiss it as predictable)
2. **Look credible** (you clearly understand both sides)
3. **Control framing** (you explain WHY they use bad-faith arguments)

**Example in practice:**

> "Before I explain our policy, let me predict what opponents will say—because I've heard it a hundred times. They'll call this 'socialism' because that's easier than defending billionaires paying 8% in taxes. They'll say 'we can't afford it' while giving $800 billion to the Pentagon. They'll claim it 'punishes success' because admitting the system is rigged would require them to fix it.
>
> Watch—I guarantee they say at least two of those things in the next 10 minutes. And when they do, remember: when you can't defend your position on the merits, you resort to buzzwords and fear-mongering.
>
> Now here's the actual policy..."

**Result:** When they DO use those attacks (and they will), the audience remembers you predicted it. The attack loses power.

---

### The "Forced Choice" Reframe

Don't let them frame the debate. Force them to answer the hard question.

**Bad (defensive):** "This isn't socialism—"

**Good (offensive):** "Should nurses pay higher tax rates than billionaires? Yes or no?"

**Bad:** "We're not trying to pack the court—"

**Good:** "Is it fair that 5 justices appointed by presidents who lost the popular vote can overturn rights for 330 million people? Yes or no?"

**The principle:** Make THEM defend the indefensible. Don't defend yourself against bad-faith attacks—attack the status quo they're protecting.

---

### The "Agree and Amplify" Technique

When they make an accusation, agree with the framing and amplify it to show how absurd it is.

**Them:** "You want to expand government!"
**You:** "Absolutely. I want to expand healthcare to every American, expand voting rights, expand public education funding. You want to expand Pentagon budgets, expand corporate subsidies, expand tax cuts for billionaires. The question isn't 'expand or not'—it's expand for WHOM?"

**Them:** "This is class warfare!"
**You:** "You're right—it is class warfare. Warren Buffett said it first: 'There's class warfare alright, but it's my class, the rich class, that's making war, and we're winning.' We're just fighting back."

---

## Final Thoughts: Offense Wins

**Stop playing defense.**

When you spend all your time defending against bad-faith attacks, you:
- Let them control the narrative
- Look uncertain and reactive
- Never get to make YOUR case

**Instead:**
1. **Predict their attacks** (inoculate your audience)
2. **Call out their bad faith** (expose their strategy)
3. **Force them to defend the indefensible** (make them explain why billionaires should pay 8% taxes)
4. **Stay on offense** (keep returning to YOUR message)

**Remember:** They attack because they CAN'T defend the status quo on the merits. When someone is defending corruption, voter suppression, and oligarchy, you've already won—you just have to make them say it out loud.
