```

```

**4. Opening Paragraph - Introduction**
- Identify yourself as a constituent (include city/ZIP)
- State purpose of letter clearly
- Make your ask specific

**5. Body Paragraphs - Why This Matters**
- Personal story or local impact (if applicable)
- Evidence/data supporting your position

- Restate your specific request
- Request a response
- Thank them for consideration

**7. Closing**
```
Sincerely,
[Your Signature]
[Your Typed Name]
```

## üé§ V. TOWN HALL BIRD-DOGGING QUESTIONS

### **How to Bird-Dog Effectively**

**Bird-dogging** = Asking tough, pointed questions at public events and recording the response

**Preparation:**
1. Research your representative's positions
2. Prepare 1-2 specific questions
3. Coordinate with others (multiple related questions = more pressure)
4. Bring recording device (phone video)
5. Plan to share response on social media

**Delivery Tips:**
- Be respectful but firm
- Ask specific yes/no questions
- Don't accept vague non-answers
- Follow up if they dodge
- Thank them for their time (even if answer is bad)

---

### **Sample Questions by Reform**

**Money in Politics:**

"Representative [NAME], you received [$AMOUNT] from [INDUSTRY] PACs last cycle. How can voters trust you to regulate that industry? Will you commit to rejecting corporate PAC money?"

"Senator [NAME], will you commit to co-sponsoring a constitutional amendment to overturn Citizens United? Yes or no?"

"Over 70% of Americans want to get money out of politics. Will you support publicly funded elections so regular people‚Äînot billionaires‚Äîcan run for office?"

---

**Voting Rights:**

"Representative [NAME], [NUMBER] eligible voters in our district aren't registered. Will you support automatic voter registration to ensure every eligible citizen can vote?"

"Why did you vote against making Election Day a federal holiday? Don't you want more people to vote?"

"Gerrymandering in our state means only 10% of seats are competitive. Will you support independent redistricting commissions?"

---

**Congressional Ethics:**

"Senator [NAME], you traded stocks in [INDUSTRY] just before voting on legislation affecting that industry. Will you support banning congressional stock trading?"

"You became a lobbyist less than a year after leaving Congress. Don't you think there should be stronger cooling-off periods to prevent the revolving door?"

---

**Electoral Reform:**

"Representative [NAME], you lost the popular vote in our state but won because of gerrymandering. Do you think that's democratic?"

"Will you support ranked-choice voting so voters can support third-party candidates without 'spoiling' elections?"

---

**Follow-Up Questions (when they dodge):**

- "That's not what I asked. Let me repeat: [YES OR NO QUESTION]"
- "Can you explain why you voted [AGAINST/FOR] this when [STAT% OF CONSTITUENTS] support it?"
- "Will you commit to a public meeting to discuss this with constituents?"
- "So you're saying you won't support [REFORM]? Can you look me in the eye and tell voters why?"

## üìÖ VI. EVENT PLANNING CHECKLISTS

### **Checklist: Community Meeting / Teach-In**

**8 Weeks Before:**
- [ ] Secure venue (library, community center, religious institution, school)
- [ ] Book guest speakers (local organizers, experts, elected officials if willing)
- [ ] Create event page (Facebook, Eventbrite, etc.)

**6 Weeks Before:**
- [ ] Design and print flyers
- [ ] Create social media graphics
- [ ] Begin outreach to potential partner organizations

**4 Weeks Before:**
- [ ] Submit press release to local media
- [ ] Distribute flyers (coffee shops, bulletin boards, libraries, community centers)
- [ ] Post on community calendars (Nextdoor, local websites, newspapers)
- [ ] Email listservs and community groups

**2 Weeks Before:**
- [ ] Confirm all speakers
- [ ] Finalize agenda
- [ ] Prepare materials (printed one-pagers, sign-in sheets, action sheets)
- [ ] Arrange tech (projector, microphone, livestream if doing)

**1 Week Before:**
- [ ] Send reminder emails to RSVPs
- [ ] Confirm venue access and setup
- [ ] Recruit volunteers (greeter, tech person, note-taker)

**Day Before:**
- [ ] Final social media push
- [ ] Test all tech
- [ ] Print any remaining materials

**Day Of:**
- [ ] Arrive early to setup
- [ ] Test microphone, projector, livestream
- [ ] Set up sign-in table
- [ ] Designate someone to record for social media

**During Event:**
- [ ] Collect contact info from attendees
- [ ] Distribute action sheets (how to get involved)
- [ ] Ask for volunteers
- [ ] Take photos/video for social media

**After Event:**
- [ ] Send thank-you email to attendees with follow-up actions
- [ ] Post event recap on social media
- [ ] Add new contacts to organizing database
- [ ] Schedule follow-up meetings with interested volunteers

---

### **Checklist: Protest / Rally**

**Planning Phase:**
- [ ] Choose date, time, location (public space or get permit if required)
- [ ] Determine messaging (what's the demand?)
- [ ] Design visuals (signs, banners, props)
- [ ] Invite partner organizations
- [ ] Check legal requirements (permit, police notification)

- [ ] Create Facebook event

## üéì HOW TO USE THIS TOOLKIT

**Getting Started:**
1. **Choose your reform focus** - Use [Quick Reference Guide](Quick_Reference_Guide.md) to select 1-2 reforms
2. **Pick 2-3 tools** from this toolkit - Don't try to do everything at once
3. **Customize** - Adapt scripts and templates to your local context
4. **Test** - Try on small scale first (10 calls, not 1000)
5. **Iterate** - Track what works, refine, scale up

**Sample Campaign Using This Toolkit:**

**Week 1:** Recruit initial team
- Use volunteer recruitment flyer
- Host kickoff meeting
- Collect contact info with sign-up sheet

**Week 2-3:** Build base
- Phone bank using call scripts
- Table at community events with sign-up sheet
- Launch online petition

**Week 4:** Apply pressure
- Email representatives using email templates
- Bird-dog town hall with prepared questions
- Submit letters to editor

**Week 5+:** Escalate and sustain
- Hold rally (use event checklist)
- Issue press release
- Continue phone banking and canvassing
