### Universal Basic Services Act

**Four Pillars of UBS:**

---

## **PILLAR 1: UNIVERSAL HEALTHCARE (Medicare for All)**

**What It Means:**
- Every American covered from birth
- No premiums, no copays, no deductibles, no networks
- Comprehensive: medical, dental, vision, mental health, long-term care, prescriptions
- Private insurance becomes supplemental only (like in UK, Australia)

**How It Works:**
- Single-payer system (government pays, providers still private)
- Negotiate drug prices (like every other country)
- Administrative savings: $500 billion/year (no billing bureaucracy)
- Preventive care emphasized (saves money long-term)

**Coverage Includes:**
- Doctor visits: Free at point of service
- Hospital care: Free
- Prescriptions: $0-10 copay max
- Mental health: Same as physical health
- Dental/vision: Included (not separate like now)
- Long-term care: Covered (nursing homes, home care)

**Cost & Funding:**
- **Total cost:** $3.5 trillion/year
- **Current spending:** $4.1 trillion/year (we already spend this much‚Äîjust badly)
- **Net savings:** $600 billion/year
- **Funded by:** Payroll tax (4% employee, 7.5% employer‚Äîless than current premiums), wealth tax, corporate tax

**Expected Outcomes:**
- 45,000 fewer deaths/year
- Bankruptcies due to medical bills: eliminated
- Small businesses freed from insurance burden
- Health outcomes: improve dramatically (preventive care)

---

## **PILLAR 2: FREE PUBLIC HIGHER EDUCATION**

**What It Means:**
- Public colleges and universities: tuition-free
- Community colleges: free (already are in some states)
- Trade schools and vocational training: free
- Student loan debt: canceled (existing $1.7T)

**How It Works:**
- Federal funding covers tuition at public institutions
- States maintain funding (MOE requirements)
- Private colleges remain private (students can still choose, pay tuition)
- Merit and need-based aid for living expenses

**Coverage Includes:**
- Tuition: $0 at public institutions
- Trade schools: Full coverage
- Apprenticeships: Paid (living stipend)
- Books/supplies: Subsidized

**Cost & Funding:**
- **Annual cost:** $80 billion/year (free public college tuition)
- **Student debt cancellation:** $1.7 trillion (one-time)
- **Funded by:** Wealth tax on top 0.1%, financial transaction tax
- **ROI:** $3-7 for every $1 spent (economic growth from educated workforce)

**Expected Outcomes:**
- College enrollment: +2 million students
- Student debt crisis: ended
- Talent unleashed (no financial barrier to education)
- Workforce quality: dramatically improves
- Intergenerational mobility: increases

---

## **PILLAR 3: UNIVERSAL BROADBAND**

**What It Means:**
- High-speed internet for every American household
- Free or low-cost ($10-20/month for high speeds)
- Fiber-to-home nationwide
- No rural/urban divide

**How It Works:**
- Public fiber networks (municipal broadband)
- Federal grants to build infrastructure
- Open-access model (ISPs compete on service, not monopolies)
- Net neutrality enforced

**Coverage Includes:**
- 100 Mbps minimum (fiber gigabit goal)
- No data caps
- Installation: free
- Equipment (router): provided or subsidized

**Cost & Funding:**
- **Infrastructure build:** $100 billion (one-time, federal)
- **Annual operations:** $20 billion/year (subsidies for low-income)
- **Funded by:** Spectrum auction revenue, telecom taxes
- **ROI:** Massive economic growth from universal access

**Expected Outcomes:**
- Digital divide: eliminated
- Rural areas: equal access
- Education: Remote learning viable
- Telehealth: Accessible everywhere
- Economic development: Rural areas revitalized

---

## **PILLAR 4: FREE PUBLIC TRANSIT**

**What It Means:**
- Buses, trains, light rail: free at point of use (no fares)
- Massively expanded service (frequency, coverage, hours)
- High-speed rail between cities
- Bike infrastructure included

**How It Works:**
- Federal funding quadruples transit budgets
- Cities eliminate fares (free-fare transit)
- Service frequency increases 3-5√ó (every 5-10 minutes)
- New routes to underserved areas
- Integrated networks (seamless transfers)

**Coverage Includes:**
- Local transit: Free (buses, subway, light rail)
- Regional transit: Free or low-cost
- High-speed rail: Affordable ($50-150 between cities)
- Bike-share: Free or low-cost

**Cost & Funding:**
- **Annual cost:** $200 billion/year (10√ó current federal transit spending)
- **Funded by:** Carbon tax, gas tax increase, congestion pricing
- **Savings:** Reduced road maintenance, healthcare (fewer accidents), environmental

**Expected Outcomes:**
- Transit ridership: triples
- Car dependency: decreases 30-50% in cities
- CO2 emissions: reduced 20% (transport is 29% of emissions)
- Traffic congestion: decreases dramatically
- Quality of life: increases (less driving, more mobility)
