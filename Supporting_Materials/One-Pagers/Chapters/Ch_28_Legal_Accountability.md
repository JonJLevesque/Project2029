## What This Chapter Covers
This chapter establishes a constitutionally sound framework for holding individuals accountable for subverting American democracy through Project 2025 and related actions between 2025-2029. It is the most legally complex and politically sensitive component of Project 2029, requiring rigorous adherence to constitutional principles, due process, and the rule of law. The core principle: accountability for genuine crimes, not political revenge.

## Top Reforms Proposed

### 1. Appoint an Independent Special Counsel
Establish a Special Counsel with full prosecutorial independence, adequate funding, and a mandate to investigate and prosecute federal crimes committed during the 2025-2029 period, modeled on the Watergate Special Prosecution Force with enhanced independence protections.

### 2. Tiered Accountability System
Implement a four-tier accountability framework: criminal prosecution for the most serious offenses (Category A principal architects and Category B active participants), civil liability, administrative sanctions, and a Truth and Reconciliation process -- ensuring proportional accountability based on culpability.

### 3. Robust Due Process Safeguards
Every accountability mechanism must satisfy constitutional requirements: First Amendment protection for political speech, Fifth and Fourteenth Amendment due process, Sixth Amendment fair trial rights, separation of powers, and equal protection. No political loyalty tests -- only evidence-based prosecution.

### 4. Clear Criminal-Political Distinction
Rigorously distinguish between protected political activity (policy preferences, regulatory philosophy, spending debates) and criminal conduct (election subversion, obstruction of justice, abuse of power, seditious conspiracy, civil rights violations). Only the latter triggers criminal accountability.

### 5. Truth and Reconciliation Commission
Establish a bipartisan commission to document the full scope of democratic subversion, provide a forum for lower-level participants to give truthful testimony in exchange for appropriate immunity, and create an authoritative public record for future generations.

### 6. Statute of Limitations Urgency
Many potential charges face 5-year statutes of limitations. Investigation and prosecution must begin immediately upon inauguration in 2029 to preserve the ability to hold accountable those who committed federal crimes during the 2025-2029 period.

## Key Legislation Proposed
- **Special Counsel Independence Act** -- Establish statutory protections for Special Counsel independence, including removal only for cause, adequate guaranteed funding, and congressional reporting requirements
- **Democratic Accountability Act** -- Create the legal framework for tiered accountability including criminal, civil, administrative, and truth-and-reconciliation tracks
- **Evidence Preservation Act** -- Mandate preservation of all government records from the 2025-2029 period, with criminal penalties for destruction, and fund secure archival infrastructure
- **Truth and Reconciliation Commission Act** -- Establish a bipartisan commission with subpoena power, witness immunity authority, and a mandate to produce a comprehensive public report
