## What This Chapter Covers
Chapters 28 and 29 addressed accountability and structural safeguards. This chapter goes further: fundamental transformation of American democracy to eliminate systemic corruption, ensure genuine political equality, and create a government truly of, by, and for the people. These reforms require constitutional amendments, generational political will, and a national movement. Project 2025 exposed not just vulnerabilities but fundamental flaws in our democratic architecture. Half-measures will not suffice.

## What Project 2025 Did / The Current Crisis
- **Corporate Dominance of Elections:** Citizens United unleashed unlimited dark money. Billions in secret spending now determine election outcomes, turning "one person, one vote" into "one dollar, one vote" and making politicians beholden to donors rather than voters.
- **Antidemocratic Electoral College:** The Electoral College allows popular vote losers to become president (2000, 2016), concentrates campaigns in 6-8 swing states, and has roots in the 3/5ths compromise. Wyoming voters have 3.6x the presidential voting power of California voters.
- **Supreme Court Captured:** Lifetime appointments created a "vacancy lottery" (Trump: 3 in 4 years, Obama: 2 in 8), allowing ideological hijacking of the judiciary for generations after a stolen Garland seat and rushed Barrett confirmation.
- **Legalized Corruption:** The revolving door between government and lobbying funnels $4+ billion annually in special interest spending. Former members of Congress immediately lobby former colleagues, and corporate access dwarfs citizen access.
- **Permanent Political Class:** Senators serving 30-50 years become detached from constituents, beholden to special interests, and obstacles to generational renewal. The 95%+ reelection rate reflects incumbency advantage, not democratic choice.

## Top Reforms Proposed

### 1. Overturn Citizens United (30th Amendment)
Amend the Constitution to establish that constitutional rights belong to natural persons only, not corporations. Empower Congress and states to regulate, limit, or prohibit contributions and expenditures for elections. Require public disclosure of all political spending.

### 2. Senate Term Limits (31st Amendment)
Limit senators to two full terms (12 years maximum). Grandfathers current senators. Prevents permanent political class while preserving institutional knowledge through strong professional staff.

### 3. Abolish the Electoral College (32nd Amendment)
Replace the Electoral College with direct popular vote. Require majority for election with runoff if needed. Interim strategy: complete the National Popular Vote Interstate Compact (currently at 209 of 270 electoral votes needed).

### 4. Supreme Court 18-Year Term Limits (33rd Amendment)
Replace lifetime appointments with 18-year staggered terms, one vacancy every two years. Each president gets predictable appointments. Reduces confirmation battles and ensures the Court reflects recent democratic will.

### 5. Comprehensive Lobbying Reform
Lifetime ban on former members of Congress lobbying their former chamber. 10-year cooling-off period for all senior government officials. Ban lobbying by companies with federal contracts over $1 million. Require FARA registration for organizations coordinating with foreign governments.

### 6. Publicly Funded Elections (Democracy Dollars)
Provide every voter a $200 voucher for political donations with 6-to-1 matching for small donations. Fund through a 0.01% financial transaction tax. Shift political power from wealthy donors to ordinary citizens.

### 7. Ranked-Choice Voting Nationwide
Break the two-party stranglehold by allowing voters to rank candidates. Eliminates "spoiler" dynamics, encourages third parties, and ensures winners have majority support.

### 8. DC and Puerto Rico Statehood
Admit DC (712,000 residents) and Puerto Rico (3.2 million citizens) as states. End taxation without representation for 3.9 million Americans. Legal basis: Article IV, Section 3, the same authority used to admit 37 states since the founding.
