## ‚úÖ THE SOLUTION

### 28th Amendment (Supreme Court Reform)

**Text:**
```
Section 1: Supreme Court Justices shall serve one 18-year term, non-renewable.

Section 2: One vacancy shall occur every two years (alternating odd/even).

Section 3: Each presidential term shall appoint exactly two Justices.

Section 4: If extraordinary vacancy (death/resignation), replacement serves
remainder of term only.

Section 5: Current Justices transition to senior status upon ratification;
serve until individual 18-year term would have expired.

Section 6: Congress may expand Court size; additional seats filled under
18-year staggered system.
```

**How 18-Year Staggered Terms Work:**

**The Math:**
- 9 Justices √∑ 2 appointments per presidential term = appointments every 2 years
- 18 years √∑ 2 = 9 Justices total on Court at any time
- Each President gets **exactly 2 appointments per 4-year term** (predictable)

**The Schedule:**
- Year 1 of presidency: Appointment #1
- Year 3 of presidency: Appointment #2
- Two-term president: 4 appointments (major influence, but not dominance)
- One-term president: 2 appointments (fair representation)

**Example Timeline (Starting 2029):**
- 2029: Biden appointee #1 joins
- 2031: Biden appointee #2 joins
- 2033: Next president appointee #1 joins
- 2035: Next president appointee #2 joins
- 2047: Biden appointee #1 term ends (18 years) ‚Üí replaced
- 2049: Biden appointee #2 term ends ‚Üí replaced

**Transition for Current Justices:**
- Calculate when their 18-year term would end (from appointment date)
- They serve until that date, then transition to "senior status"
- Senior status = can hear lower court cases, mentor, write, but not vote on Supreme Court
- Phased transition over 15-20 years as current Justices' terms expire

**If Justice Dies/Resigns Mid-Term:**
- President appoints replacement
- Replacement serves **remainder of 18-year term only** (not new 18 years)
- Preserves regular schedule (appointments still every 2 years on schedule)

**If Court Expanded (e.g., to 13 Justices):**
- Add 4 seats immediately
- Those 4 appointments staggered over 4 years (one per year)
- After that, back to regular schedule (appointments every 2 years)

## üí¨ TALKING POINTS

**"Doesn't lifetime tenure protect judicial independence?"**
‚Üí 18 years is PLENTY to protect independence. Federal judges already have mandatory retirement at 70 (senior status). What lifetime tenure actually does is create random vacancies, partisan lotteries, and aging Justices clinging to seats. 18 years = independence + accountability.

**"Is this constitutional without an amendment?"**
‚Üí Debatable. Some scholars say Congress could do this by statute (Justices move to "senior status" after 18 years). But constitutional amendment is cleaner, more legitimate, and prevents legal challenges. Worth doing right.

**"Won't this make the Court more political?"**
‚Üí Court is ALREADY political (Bush v. Gore, Citizens United, Dobbs). Current system made it hyperpartisan through luck-based appointments. Regular predictable appointments REDUCE politicization‚Äîevery president gets fair share, no supermajorities, Court reflects recent voters.

**"What about judicial experience and wisdom?"**
‚Üí 18 years on Supreme Court = massive experience. Most Justices are appointed in their 50s, so 18-year term = serve until late 60s/early 70s. Peak wisdom years. Plus they can serve on lower courts afterward (senior status). We don't lose their wisdom.

**"Why 18 years specifically?"**
‚Üí Math: 9 Justices, 4-year presidential term, need system where every president gets 2 appointments. 18 years √∑ 2 appointments per term = 9 Justices. Perfect. Also: long enough for independence (not worried about next job), short enough for accountability (not 40-year tenure).
