## ‚úÖ THE SOLUTION

### Political Deepfake Prohibition and AI Transparency Act

---

## **PART I: BAN POLITICAL DEEPFAKES**

**Criminal Prohibition:**

**It is a federal crime to:**
- Create, distribute, or promote AI-generated media (video, audio, image) of political candidates without clear, prominent disclosure
- Create deepfakes of candidates with intent to deceive voters
- Distribute deepfakes within 60 days of election without watermark/disclosure

**Penalties:**

**For Creators:**
- First offense: $250,000 fine + 2 years prison
- Repeat offense: $1 million fine + 5 years prison
- If affects election outcome: 10 years prison

**For Distributors (Platforms):**
- Fail to remove flagged deepfake within 24 hours: $1 million/day
- Fail to implement detection systems: $10 million fine
- Knowing distribution: $100 million fine + potential Section 230 loss

**Exceptions (Allowed with Clear Labeling):**
- Satire/parody (clearly labeled)
- News commentary (clearly labeled as "simulation" or "deepfake")
- Educational purposes
- **But ALL must have prominent "AI-GENERATED" watermark**

---

## **PART II: MANDATORY DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS**

**All AI-Generated Political Content Must:**

1. **Visual Watermark:**
   - "AI-GENERATED" or "SYNTHETIC MEDIA" in corner of image/video
   - Cannot be removed or obscured
   - Large enough to be readable (minimum 5% of frame)
   - Contrasting color (visible against background)

2. **Audio Disclosure:**
   - "This audio was created using artificial intelligence" played at beginning and end
   - Cannot be cut or edited out
   - Clear, understandable voice

3. **Metadata:**
   - AI generation disclosed in file metadata
   - Source/tool used documented
   - Creator information (if applicable)
   - Timestamped and signed (blockchain or equivalent)

4. **Platform Labeling:**
   - Social media must add warning label
   - "This content was AI-generated" banner
   - Link to fact-checking resources
   - Reduce algorithmic amplification

---

## **PART III: DETECTION & ENFORCEMENT**

**Federal AI Election Integrity Task Force:**

**Composition:**
- 500 FBI agents + 200 technical experts
- Real-time monitoring of political content
- AI detection tools (constantly updated)
- Coordination with platforms and intelligence agencies

**Powers:**
- **Immediate takedown authority** for unlabeled deepfakes
- **Criminal investigation** of deepfake creators
- **Emergency injunctions** to prevent spread
- **Coordinate with states** for state/local elections

**Platform Responsibilities:**

**Social Media/Video Platforms Must:**
- Implement AI detection systems (state-of-the-art)
- Remove unlabeled political deepfakes within 24 hours of flagging
- Provide easy reporting mechanism
- Preserve evidence for prosecution
- Cooperation with law enforcement

**Penalties for Non-Compliance:**
- $1 million per day per violation
- Can lose Section 230 protection (huge incentive)
- Criminal liability for knowing violations

**Pre-Election Monitoring (60 Days Before):**
- Enhanced monitoring (automated + human review)
- Real-time detection and flagging
- Emergency response team (24/7)
- Coordination with campaigns (alert candidates to deepfakes of them)

---

## **PART IV: AUTHENTICATION INFRASTRUCTURE**

**Content Authentication Standards:**

**"Verified Real" System:**
- Candidates can record authenticated content
- Digital signature + blockchain verification
- Voters can verify authenticity via QR code or link
- "This video is verified authentic by [candidate]" watermark

**Distributed Ledger:**
- All authenticated political content registered
- Immutable record of what's real
- Public can check if content is authenticated
- Deepfakes can't replicate authentication

**Tools for Public:**
- Free deepfake detection tools (government-provided)
- Browser extensions (flag potential deepfakes)
- Mobile apps (scan video to check authenticity)
- Educational campaigns (how to spot deepfakes)

**"Won't bad actors just ignore the law?"**
‚Üí Some will. But: (1) Platforms must remove or face huge fines‚Äîmost deepfakes distributed via platforms. (2) Criminal penalties deter many. (3) Public educated to spot deepfakes.
