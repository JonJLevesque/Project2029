## ‚úÖ THE SOLUTION

### Paper Ballot Trail Mandate Act

**Federal Requirement:**

**All federal elections (President, Senate, House) must use:**

1. **Paper Ballots (Human-Readable)**
   - Voter-verified paper ballots
   - Human-readable (not just barcodes or QR codes)
   - Voter reviews ballot before casting
   - Paper ballot is official record

2. **Options for Generating Paper Ballots:**

   **Option A: Hand-Marked Paper Ballots**
   - Voter fills out ballot by hand (pen/pencil)
   - Fed into scanner that counts votes
   - Paper ballot saved in secure box
   - **Most secure, most trustworthy**

   **Option B: Ballot-Marking Devices (BMDs)**
   - Voter uses machine to make selections (accessible for disabilities)
   - Machine PRINTS human-readable paper ballot
   - Voter reviews printed ballot before casting
   - Paper ballot fed into scanner and saved
   - **Accessible option with paper trail**

   **Option C: Mail-In Paper Ballots**
   - Voter receives paper ballot by mail
   - Fills out by hand
   - Returns by mail or drop box
   - **Convenient and secure**

3. **Risk-Limiting Audits (Mandatory)**
   - Hand-count random sample of paper ballots after election
   - Compare to machine count
   - If discrepancy: expand audit
   - Statistical confidence election was counted correctly
   - **Proof that machines counted correctly**

4. **Recounts Use Paper Ballots**
   - If close election or dispute: recount paper ballots
   - Cannot rely on re-running machine count
   - Hand count is backup
   - **Paper = insurance policy**

5. **Ballot Security Requirements**
   - Paper ballots stored securely (tamper-evident seals)
   - Chain of custody documented
   - Ballots preserved for 22 months (federal law)
   - Random audits ensure integrity

**Ban Paperless Machines:**
- No direct-recording electronic (DRE) machines without paper trail
- Existing paperless machines must be replaced by 2026
- Federal funding provided for replacement
- No exceptions

**Accessibility Maintained:**
- Ballot-marking devices (BMDs) for voters with disabilities
- Audio ballots
- Touchscreen interfaces
- But MUST produce human-readable paper ballot
- Accessibility does NOT require paperless voting

## üí¨ TALKING POINTS

**"Aren't electronic machines more modern and efficient?"**
‚Üí "Modern" doesn't mean "secure." Paper ballots with electronic SCANNERS are both modern AND secure‚Äîmachines count fast, paper provides proof. Paperless machines are like banks with no receipts‚Äîefficient until something goes wrong, then you're screwed. Paper = insurance.

**"Can't we just improve cybersecurity on paperless machines?"**
‚Üí NO. Security experts universally say: Nothing connected to humans can be 100% secure. Paper ballots CANNOT be hacked remotely‚Äîyou'd need physical access to millions of paper ballots. Machines can be hacked in minutes. Why gamble democracy on cybersecurity when paper is unhackable?

**"Don't paper ballots slow things down?"**
‚Üí Barely. Optical scanners count paper ballots almost as fast as paperless machines. Results might be delayed by 1-2 hours on election night. WORTH IT for secure elections. Plus, mail-in paper ballots (counted before Election Day) speed things up.

**"What about recounts‚Äîdon't paper ballots make them harder?"**
‚Üí OPPOSITE. Paperless machines CAN'T be recounted (no paper trail). You'd just re-run the same potentially hacked machine. Paper ballots can ALWAYS be recounted‚Äîhand count is always available. That's the point.

**"What about accessibility for disabled voters?"**
‚Üí Ballot-marking devices (BMDs) provide full accessibility‚Äîaudio, touchscreen, etc.‚ÄîAND produce paper ballots. You can have both accessibility and security. No tradeoff required. Plus, nearly all voters can use hand-marked paper ballots.
