## **COMPONENT 1: COMPREHENSIVE CIVICS CURRICULUM (K-12)**

**What It Includes:**

**Elementary (K-5):**
- Community civics (local government, community roles)
- Democratic values (fairness, equality, rights/responsibilities)
- Civil discourse basics (listening, disagreeing respectfully)
- Current events discussions (age-appropriate)

**Middle School (6-8):**
- US government structure (three branches, federalism)
- Constitution and Bill of Rights
- Voting and elections
- Civil rights history
- State and local government
- Mock elections and student government

**High School (9-12):**
- **Required: 1 full year of US Government** (all states)
- **Required: 1 semester of Comparative Political Systems**
- **Required: 1 semester of Media Literacy**
- **Required: 1 semester of Current Events & Critical Thinking**
- Mock trials, debates, simulations
- Service learning (community engagement)

**Graduation Requirement:**
- Pass civics proficiency exam (like citizenship test)
- Complete 40 hours community service
- Demonstrate media literacy skills

---

## **COMPONENT 2: MEDIA LITERACY EDUCATION**

**What Students Learn:**

1. **Source Evaluation:**
   - Who published this? (Author credentials, publication reputation)
   - What's their motive? (Bias, financial incentive, agenda)
   - Is it verified? (Multiple sources, fact-checking)
   - When was it published? (Currency of information)

2. **Identifying Disinformation:**
   - Emotional manipulation tactics
   - Logical fallacies
   - Fake news vs. real news
   - Deepfakes and AI-generated content
   - Bot accounts and coordinated campaigns

3. **Digital Citizenship:**
   - Privacy and data protection
   - Social media algorithms (how they work, how they manipulate)
   - Online harassment and trolling
   - Responsible content creation and sharing

4. **News Consumption:**
   - Differences: news, opinion, analysis, entertainment
   - Reading across ideological spectrum
   - Fact-checking tools and techniques
   - Understanding journalistic standards

**Curriculum:**
- Integrated into English/Language Arts (K-12)
- Standalone course in high school (1 semester required)
- Ongoing skills practice across all subjects

---

## **COMPONENT 3: CRITICAL THINKING ACROSS CURRICULUM**

**Skills Taught:**

1. **Argumentation:**
   - Constructing logical arguments
   - Identifying assumptions
   - Evaluating evidence
   - Recognizing fallacies

2. **Scientific Reasoning:**
   - Hypothesis testing
   - Understanding correlation vs. causation
   - Reading scientific studies
   - Distinguishing science from pseudoscience

3. **Historical Thinking:**
   - Analyzing primary sources
   - Understanding context
   - Recognizing historical bias
   - Drawing informed conclusions

4. **Socratic Seminars:**
   - Structured discussions
   - Questioning and inquiry
   - Building on others' ideas
   - Changing minds based on evidence

**Implementation:**
- Integrated into ALL subjects (not just social studies)
- Emphasis on "how to think," not "what to think"
- Assessment: demonstrated reasoning skills, not memorization

---

## **COMPONENT 4: DEMOCRATIC VALUES & CIVIL DISCOURSE**

**Values Emphasized:**

1. **Core Democratic Principles:**
   - Equality (political, legal, social)
   - Liberty (freedom with responsibility)
   - Justice (fairness, rule of law)
   - Civic participation (rights and duties)

2. **Pluralism & Tolerance:**
   - Multiple perspectives validity
   - Agreeing to disagree
   - Respectful debate
   - Finding common ground

3. **Civil Discourse Skills:**
   - Active listening
   - Steel-manning (argue opponent's best case)
   - Changing minds with evidence, not emotion
   - Admitting when wrong

**Practice:**
- Class debates (structured, moderated)
- Deliberative forums (discuss real issues)
- Conflict resolution training
- Service learning (work with diverse communities)
