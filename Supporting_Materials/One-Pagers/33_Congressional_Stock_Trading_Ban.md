# Reform #33: Ban Stock Trading by Elected Officials

## The Elected Officials Financial Integrity Act

**The Problem:** Members of Congress write the laws, fund the agencies, and oversee the industries that move financial markets. They receive classified intelligence briefings, advance knowledge of legislation, and private access to agency officials. They are legally permitted to trade individual stocks while holding all of this privileged information. The result is a system of institutionalized corruption so brazen that multiple studies have found congressional stock portfolios **outperform the market by 6-12% annually** — a statistical impossibility under conditions of genuine ignorance.

**The Solution:** A complete ban on individual stock trading by all elected federal officials, their spouses, and dependent children — with mandatory divestment into blind trusts within 90 days of taking office, criminal penalties for violations, and real-time public disclosure of all financial holdings.

## What the Law Currently Says — and Why It Fails

**The STOCK Act (2012)** — the Stop Trading on Congressional Knowledge Act — was passed with great fanfare and near-unanimous votes. It declared that members of Congress are subject to insider trading laws and required them to disclose stock trades within **45 days**.

**What it actually does:** Almost nothing.

- The **penalty for late disclosure is $200.** A senator who trades on advance knowledge of a drug approval and makes $500,000 can pay $200 and walk away.
- **There is no prohibition** on trading in industries a member directly regulates through committee assignments.
- **Enforcement depends on the SEC** — which has never prosecuted a sitting member of Congress for STOCK Act violations.
- **Disclosures are filed in PDFs** that are deliberately difficult to aggregate and analyze.
- **Spouses and staff** have substantial loopholes.
- The **45-day window** is wide enough to complete and profit from any trade before disclosure occurs.

The STOCK Act was designed to look like reform while preserving the underlying corruption. It succeeded at both.

## The Documented Corruption

### The COVID Trades (2020)

On **January 24, 2020**, the Senate received a classified intelligence briefing on the emerging COVID-19 pandemic — including projections of market disruption and supply chain collapse. In the weeks following:

- **Sen. Richard Burr (R-NC):** Sold $628,000-$1.72M in stocks — including hotel and hospitality stocks — days after the briefing. The FBI seized his phone. DOJ ultimately declined to prosecute.
- **Sen. Kelly Loeffler (R-GA):** Made 29 transactions totaling between $1.3M-$3.1M in sales, including airline stocks, in the weeks following the briefing. Also purchased stock in a company selling remote work software.
- **Sen. Jim Inhofe (R-OK):** Sold between $180,000-$400,000 in stocks after the briefing.
- **Sen. Dianne Feinstein (D-CA):** Her husband sold $1.5-$6M in biotech stocks.

**Criminal prosecutions:** Zero. The DOJ declined all cases.

### The Nancy Pelosi Pattern

Speaker Nancy Pelosi's husband Paul Pelosi — using capital legally available to him — executed trades in companies directly affected by legislation the Speaker controlled. His semiconductor stock trades preceded the CHIPS Act by weeks. His Nvidia options trade in 2021 returned over **$5M**.

Pelosi is not unique. She is documented. Dozens of members engage in identical behavior with less scrutiny.

### The Committee Assignment Problem

Members of the **Senate Intelligence Committee** receive classified briefings on national security threats, cyberattacks, and foreign government actions — all of which move markets. Members of the **Senate Finance Committee** receive advance knowledge of tax legislation. Members of the **Senate Health Committee** know which drugs the FDA is about to approve or deny.

All of them are legally permitted to trade stocks in the industries they oversee. This is not a bug in the system. It is the system.

## The Reform: What the Ban Requires

### Core Prohibition

**No elected federal official** — President, Vice President, member of the Senate, member of the House of Representatives — may:
- Purchase or sell individual stocks, bonds, or securities of any company
- Purchase or sell stock options, futures, or derivatives on individual securities
- Hold a financial interest in any company subject to legislation, regulation, or investigation by their committee or chamber

### Who It Covers

| Official | Covered |
|----------|---------|
| All 535 members of Congress | Yes |
| President and Vice President | Yes |
| Spouses and dependent children | Yes |
| Senior congressional staff | Yes |
| Cabinet secretaries and agency heads | Yes (existing ethics rules strengthened) |
| Federal judges and Supreme Court justices | Yes — extended by companion legislation |

### Required Divestment

All covered individuals must divest individual stock holdings into:
- **Diversified mutual funds** (no sector-specific funds)
- **Index funds** (S&P 500, total market)
- **U.S. Treasury bonds**
- **Qualified blind trusts** administered by an independent trustee with no communication to the official about holdings

**Timeline:** 90 days from taking office (or from date of enactment for current officeholders).

### Penalties

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Failure to divest within 90 days | Mandatory forfeiture of all gains + $50,000 civil fine |
| Trading prohibited securities | Forfeiture of gains + criminal referral to DOJ |
| Insider trading conviction | Up to 20 years federal imprisonment (18 USC § 1348) |
| Late or false disclosure | $10,000 per violation (up from $200) |
| Spousal/dependent violation | Same as principal |

### Disclosure Reform

- **Real-time disclosure** within **48 hours** of any transaction by spouse or dependent (current: 45 days)
- **Machine-readable format** — all disclosures filed in structured data (JSON/CSV), not PDFs
- **Public searchable database** maintained by an independent ethics body — not Congress itself
- **SEC cross-referencing** — automatic flagging when disclosed trades correlate with legislative activity or classified briefings

## Legal Authority

**Congressional authority to regulate its own members:** Article I, Section 5 grants each chamber power to determine its own rules and discipline members. Congress has plenary authority to impose ethics and financial conduct requirements on its members.

**For executive branch officials:** 18 USC § 208 (conflicts of interest), 5 CFR Part 2635 (standards of ethical conduct), and the Ethics in Government Act (5 USC App. 4) already impose significant restrictions — the reform strengthens and closes loopholes in existing law.

**Insider trading law:** 15 USC § 78j and 17 CFR 240.10b-5 prohibit securities fraud based on material nonpublic information. The STOCK Act (15 USC § 78u-1) explicitly applies these laws to Congress. Stronger enforcement does not require new legal authority — it requires political will.

**The constitutional question:** No court has ever held that members of Congress have a First Amendment or other constitutional right to trade stocks. The "right" to insider trading does not exist in constitutional law.

## Extension: Federal Judges and the Supreme Court

The judicial branch is the most obvious gap. Federal judges — including Supreme Court justices — are not covered by the STOCK Act. Yet:

- **Justice Clarence Thomas** received undisclosed travel and gifts from Harlan Crow, a real estate developer with interests before the Court, for over two decades. ProPublica documented at least **$4M** in unreported benefits.
- **Justice Samuel Alito** accepted luxury travel from Paul Singer, a hedge fund manager with cases pending before the Court.
- Supreme Court justices are subject to **no enforceable ethics code** and no financial disclosure requirement that covers privately held interests.

The Elected Officials Financial Integrity Act should include a companion judicial provision mandating:
- Full financial disclosure for all federal judges, including Supreme Court justices
- Recusal requirements when a justice holds stock in a party before the Court
- An enforceable Supreme Court ethics code with investigation authority

## The Political Reality

This reform **polls at 76% support** — including majority support among Republicans, Democrats, and independents. It is one of the few issues where the American public is unified across partisan lines.

The only constituency that opposes it: members of Congress who profit from the current system.

**Why it hasn't passed:** The STOCK Act was designed to defuse public anger without actually ending the corruption. The Ban Stock Trading Act (introduced in multiple sessions) has been bottled up in committee by leadership in both parties. The legislators who would need to vote for the ban are the same legislators who would lose the most from it.

This is precisely the kind of reform that can only pass when political will exists to force it — through constituent pressure, primary threats, and public accountability.
