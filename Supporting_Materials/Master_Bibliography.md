## ๐ III. SUPREME COURT CASES

### **Campaign Finance**

**Buckley v. Valeo**, 424 U.S. 1 (1976)
- Held: Campaign contribution limits constitutional; expenditure limits violate First Amendment
- Established "money is speech" doctrine
- Upheld disclosure requirements

**Citizens United v. Federal Election Commission**, 558 U.S. 310 (2010)
- Held: Corporate independent expenditures protected by First Amendment
- Overturned *Austin v. Michigan Chamber of Commerce*
- Created Super PAC era

**McCutcheon v. Federal Election Commission**, 572 U.S. 185 (2014)
- Held: Aggregate contribution limits unconstitutional
- Plurality opinion by Chief Justice Roberts
- Further weakened campaign finance restrictions

**SpeechNow.org v. FEC**, 599 F.3d 686 (D.C. Cir. 2010)
- Applied *Citizens United* to create Super PACs
- Held: No limits on contributions to independent expenditure committees

---

### **Voting Rights**

**Baker v. Carr**, 369 U.S. 186 (1962)
- Held: Redistricting subject to judicial review
- Established one person, one vote principle foundation

**Reynolds v. Sims**, 377 U.S. 533 (1964)
- Held: State legislative districts must be roughly equal in population
- "One person, one vote" principle

**Shelby County v. Holder**, 570 U.S. 529 (2013)
- Held: Section 4(b) of Voting Rights Act unconstitutional (coverage formula)
- Effectively nullified Section 5 preclearance requirement
- Major setback for voting rights enforcement

**Brnovich v. Democratic National Committee**, 594 U.S. ___ (2021)
- Held: Arizona voting restrictions do not violate Voting Rights Act Section 2
- Made it harder to challenge voting restrictions

**Bush v. Gore**, 531 U.S. 98 (2000)
- Decided 2000 presidential election
- Equal Protection and Due Process issues in Florida recount
- Controversial 5-4 decision

---

### **Executive Power**

**Youngstown Sheet & Tube Co. v. Sawyer**, 343 U.S. 579 (1952) (Steel Seizure Case)
- Held: President lacks inherent authority to seize private property
- Justice Jackson's concurrence: three-tier framework for executive power
  - Tier 1: Acting with Congressional authorization (maximum power)
  - Tier 2: Congressional silence (twilight zone)
  - Tier 3: Acting contrary to Congress (lowest ebb)

**United States v. Nixon**, 418 U.S. 683 (1974)
- Held: Executive privilege limited, not absolute
- President must comply with subpoena in criminal investigation
- Led to Nixon resignation

**Clinton v. Jones**, 520 U.S. 681 (1997)
- Held: Sitting president can be sued for unofficial acts
- President does not have temporary immunity from civil damages litigation

**Trump v. Vance**, 591 U.S. ___ (2020)
- Held: President not immune from state criminal subpoena
- Must comply with grand jury subpoena for financial records

**Trump v. United States**, 603 U.S. ___ (2024)
- Held: Presidents have absolute immunity for core constitutional powers, presumptive immunity for official acts
- Controversial 6-3 decision expanding presidential immunity
- Major expansion of executive power

---

### **Separation of Powers**

**INS v. Chadha**, 462 U.S. 919 (1983)
- Held: Legislative veto unconstitutional
- Reinforced presentment and bicameralism requirements

**Morrison v. Olson**, 487 U.S. 654 (1988)
- Held: Independent Counsel Act constitutional
- Ethics in Government Act restrictions on presidential removal power upheld

**Seila Law LLC v. Consumer Financial Protection Bureau**, 591 U.S. ___ (2020)
- Held: CFPB's single-director structure with for-cause removal protection unconstitutional
- President must have removal power over executive officers

---

### **First Amendment**

**New York Times Co. v. United States**, 403 U.S. 713 (1971) (Pentagon Papers)
- Held: Government cannot impose prior restraint on publication without extraordinary justification
- Strong protection for press freedom

**Red Lion Broadcasting Co. v. FCC**, 395 U.S. 367 (1969)
- Upheld Fairness Doctrine against First Amendment challenge
- Scarcity rationale for broadcast regulation
- Distinguished from print media (see *Miami Herald*)

**FCC v. Fox Television Stations, Inc.**, 567 U.S. 239 (2012)
- Held: FCC's indecency policy void for vagueness
- Did not reach First Amendment question

---

### **Equal Protection**

**Brown v. Board of Education**, 347 U.S. 483 (1954)
- Overturned *Plessy v. Ferguson*
- Separate is inherently unequal
- Foundation for civil rights enforcement

**Loving v. Virginia**, 388 U.S. 1 (1967)
- Struck down interracial marriage bans
- Equal Protection and Due Process

**Obergefell v. Hodges**, 576 U.S. 644 (2015)
- Same-sex marriage constitutional right
- Equal Protection and Due Process
