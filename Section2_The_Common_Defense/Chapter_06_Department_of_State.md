# Chapter 6: Department of State

## Executive Summary

Project 2025 abandoned American diplomatic leadership, withdrew from international agreements, undermined career Foreign Service officers, and prioritized authoritarians over democracies. This chapter outlines how to restore diplomatic leadership, rebuild alliances, rejoin international agreements, and prioritize democracy and human rights in foreign policy.

## The Project 2025 Damage Assessment

### Diplomatic Destruction
- Withdrew from Paris Climate Agreement, WHO, UN Human Rights Council
- Abandoned allies (NATO weakened, Asian alliances strained)
- Embraced authoritarians (Russia, Saudi Arabia, North Korea)
- Career diplomats purged or sidelined
- USAID humanitarian mission undermined
- Multilateral institutions weakened

### Impact on American Leadership
- Loss of global credibility and soft power
- Allies turned to other partners
- Authoritarian regimes emboldened
- Human rights backsliding worldwide
- Climate crisis accelerated

## Immediate Reversal Actions (Days 1-100)

### Day One Executive Actions
1. Rejoin Paris Climate Agreement
2. Rejoin World Health Organization
3. Recommit to NATO Article 5
4. Restore career Foreign Service leadership
5. End Muslim travel ban remnants
6. Restore refugee admission levels

### Alliance Restoration
- NATO summit within first 100 days
- Asian ally consultations (Japan, South Korea, Philippines)
- Rebuild European partnerships
- Restore Latin American engagement
- Strengthen AUKUS and Quad partnerships

## Democratic Renewal Agenda

### Diplomatic Leadership Restoration

**Career Foreign Service Rebuilding:**
- Restore merit-based ambassadorial appointments
- End political donor ambassadorships for critical posts
- Rebuild Foreign Service morale and capacity
- Restore diplomatic training and expertise
- Invest in language skills and cultural competency

**Institutional Reforms:**
- Depoliticize policy planning staff
- Restore independent intelligence analysis at State
- Strengthen consular services
- Modernize diplomatic technology and security
- Adequate resources and staffing

### Democracy and Human Rights Priority

**Core Principles:**
- Human rights central to foreign policy
- Support democratic movements worldwide
- Accountability for authoritarian abuses
- Protection of civil society and free press
- LGBTQ+ rights as human rights

**Mechanisms:**
- Restore Bureau of Democracy, Human Rights, and Labor authority
- Global Magnitsky Act enforcement
- Sanctions on human rights abusers
- Support for political prisoners
- Democracy summit convening

### Multilateral Engagement

**UN and International Institutions:**
- Rejoin UN Human Rights Council with reforms
- Pay UN dues fully and on time
- Reform international institutions where needed
- Support UN peacekeeping
- International Criminal Court cooperation

**Climate Leadership:**
- Paris Agreement implementation and strengthening
- International climate finance commitment
- Green technology partnerships
- Just transition support for developing nations

**Global Health:**
- WHO leadership restoration
- Pandemic preparedness cooperation
- Vaccine equity and global health security
- Strengthened international health regulations

### Regional Strategies

**Europe:**
- NATO revitalization
- EU partnership strengthening
- Ukraine support continuation
- Transatlantic trade cooperation
- Democratic backsliding engagement (Hungary, Poland reforms)

**Indo-Pacific:**
- China strategy: competitive but not Cold War
- Alliance network strengthening
- ASEAN engagement
- Pacific Island partnerships
- Trade and economic integration

**Middle East:**
- Two-state solution advancement
- Iran nuclear diplomacy
- Saudi human rights accountability
- Iraq and Afghanistan partnership (not occupation)
- Yemen humanitarian crisis resolution

**Latin America:**
- Partnership of equals approach
- Democracy support in Venezuela, Nicaragua
- Immigration root cause investment
- Climate and development cooperation
- Drug policy reform (harm reduction)

**Africa:**
- Partnership not paternalism
- Democracy and governance support
- Health and development investment
- Climate adaptation assistance
- Counter-terrorism without militarization
