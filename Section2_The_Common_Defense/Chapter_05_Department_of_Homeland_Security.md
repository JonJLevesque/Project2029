# Chapter 5: Department of Homeland Security

## Executive Summary

The Department of Homeland Security was weaponized under Project 2025 to target political opponents, suppress dissent, and violate civil liberties. This chapter outlines comprehensive reforms to restore DHS to its proper mission: protecting Americans from genuine threats while upholding constitutional rights, improving oversight of agencies like ICE and CBP, and depoliticizing domestic security functions.

## The Project 2025 Damage Assessment

### What Project 2025 Did

**Politicization of Domestic Security**
- Weaponized DHS against political opponents and protesters
- Used federal agents to suppress First Amendment activities
- Deployed paramilitary forces against peaceful demonstrations
- Created "enemy lists" of journalists and activists
- Expanded surveillance of domestic political groups

**Immigration Enforcement Abuses**
- Mass deportations without due process
- Family separations reinstituted and expanded
- Detention conditions violated human rights standards
- ICE and CBP operated without meaningful oversight
- Asylum system destroyed
- Legal immigration pathways blocked

**Civil Liberties Violations**
- Warrantless surveillance expansion
- No-fly list weaponization
- Border searches of electronic devices without cause
- Fusion center abuses targeting political groups
- First Amendment zone violations

**Institutional Damage**
- Career professionals purged for political reasons
- Scientific and expert advice ignored
- Inspector General independence undermined
- Congressional oversight evaded
- Whistleblower retaliation
- Interagency coordination breakdown

## Immediate Reversal Actions (Days 1-100)

### Day One Executive Orders

**1. Restore Civil Liberties Protections**
- Immediately end all politically motivated investigations
- Prohibit surveillance of First Amendment activities
- Restore due process protections
- End warrantless electronic device searches at borders

**2. Immigration System Reform**
- Halt mass deportation operations
- End family separations permanently
- Restore asylum system
- Improve detention conditions to meet human rights standards
- Reinstate legal immigration pathways

**3. Depoliticize DHS Operations**
- Remove political appointees from operational positions
- Restore career professional leadership
- Reestablish scientific and expert advisory processes
- Strengthen whistleblower protections

### First 100 Days Priorities

1. Comprehensive review of all DHS operations since 2025
2. Restore Inspector General independence and authority
3. Implement robust oversight mechanisms for ICE and CBP
4. Rebuild relationships with immigrant communities
5. Restore interagency coordination
6. Begin prosecutions for civil rights violations where warranted

## Democratic Renewal Agenda

### Core Mission Restoration

**Refocus on Genuine Threats**
- Counterterrorism based on intelligence, not politics
- Cybersecurity protection for critical infrastructure
- Natural disaster preparedness and response
- Border security that respects human rights
- Protection from foreign interference

**End Political Weaponization**
- Prohibit investigations based on political viewpoint
- Strict limits on domestic surveillance
- Congressional notification requirements
- Independent oversight of all operations
- Whistleblower protections and rewards

### Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) Reform

**Comprehensive ICE Restructuring**

**End Enforcement Abuses:**
- Prioritize genuine public safety threats, not families
- Restore prosecutorial discretion
- End workplace raids
- Improve coordination with local law enforcement (not coercion)
- Implement community policing model

**Due Process Restoration:**
- Ensure legal representation for all proceedings
- End detention quotas
- Improve conditions in detention facilities
- Alternatives to detention for non-dangerous individuals
- Expedite immigration court proceedings

**Accountability Mechanisms:**
- Independent oversight board for ICE operations
- Body cameras required for all enforcement actions
- Complaint mechanisms with real consequences
- Regular audits and reporting
- Criminal prosecution for rights violations

### Customs and Border Protection (CBP) Reform

**Border Security with Human Rights**

**Operational Reforms:**
- Professional training emphasizing rights and dignity
- Technology over walls (sensors, drones, monitoring)
- Humanitarian response capacity at borders
- Medical care standards enforcement
- End of family separations (codified in law)

**Accountability and Oversight:**
- Independent inspector general with expanded authority
- Mandatory body cameras for all agents
- Use of force restrictions and review
- Complaint investigation by independent body
- Regular human rights audits

**Asylum System Restoration:**
- Rebuild asylum processing capacity
- Restore credible fear interviews
- Legal representation access
- Humanitarian parole authority
- Safe third country agreement reforms

### Transportation Security Administration (TSA) Reform

**Security with Civil Liberties**
- End no-fly list abuses and provide due process
- Limit collection and retention of passenger data
- Improve screening technology to reduce invasiveness
- Complaint mechanisms that work
- Religious and disability accommodation protections

### Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA)

**Strengthen Cyber Defenses**
- Expand critical infrastructure protection
- Election security coordination with states
- Disinformation threat identification (foreign only, not domestic politics)
- Private sector partnership without surveillance
- Incident response capacity building

### Secret Service Reform

**Presidential Protection Without Politics**
- Restore apolitical culture
- Professional standards for all assignments
- Resource adequacy for protective mission
- Investigative mission balance
- Whistleblower protections for reporting misconduct

### Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA)

**Disaster Response Without Discrimination**
- End political considerations in disaster aid
- Rebuild professional emergency management capacity
- Climate adaptation planning
- Equity in disaster response and recovery
- State and local partnership restoration

### Coast Guard

**Maritime Security and Safety**
- Restore focus on search and rescue
- Drug interdiction professionalization
- Port security without civil liberties violations
- Environmental protection mission restoration
- Military readiness for national defense role

## Constitutional and Legal Framework

**Constitutional Protections:**
- First Amendment: Speech, assembly, press, religion
- Fourth Amendment: Searches, seizures, warrants, probable cause
- Fifth Amendment: Due process, equal protection (incorporated)
- Sixth Amendment: Right to counsel in criminal proceedings
- Eighth Amendment: Humane detention conditions
- Fourteenth Amendment: Equal protection, due process

**Statutory Framework:**

- Immigration and Nationality Act
- Administrative Procedure Act
- Privacy Act
- Freedom of Information Act

**International Obligations:**

## Conclusion

The Department of Homeland Security must return to its founding mission: protecting Americans from genuine threats while upholding the constitutional rights that define our democracy. Project 2025 transformed DHS into a tool of political repression and civil liberties violation—the opposite of its proper role.

By implementing these reforms, we will:
- Restore civil liberties protections
- End immigration enforcement abuses
- Rebuild trust with communities
- Focus on genuine threats
- Ensure accountability for violations
- Strengthen America's security through respect for rights

A DHS that respects rights is stronger and more effective than one that violates them. These reforms will make America both safer and freer.
